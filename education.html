My love for teaching started during my time at the University of Florida, where I facilitated weekly workshops for a membership of 30 on topics such as cybersecurity, web development, user experience, blockchain, and data analytics. I targeted leading technology and associated career opportunities, such as "Day in the Life of a Data Analyst" to allow students to explore career paths while gaining technical knowledge to showcase to recruiters.

Recent Speaking & Mentorship Opportunities

  • As a Speaker for Women Who Code's Data Science Track, I've presented webinars on Data Visualization best practices and PowerBI tutorials for a total of 100 data enthusiasts.
  • Additionally, I "pass the mic" to empowering women in Data Science on a monthly basis for Instagram live interviews and I host virtual community events such as "Bytes & Brews" to kickstart conversations and connections between members.
  • I've presented workshops on the role of audit in ethical artificial intelligence (AI) to 50 colleagues. Additionally, I assisted in Protiviti's development of an audit methodology for ethical AI.
  • Assisted students in the product development and creative execution of their mobile application for 20+ hours of the Afrofuturism Virtual Hackathon sponsored by Black Girls CODE.
  • As a workshop facilitator for Girls Who Code, I facilitated Design Thinking focused workshops on career opportunities in technology for 25 students by exploring the design, functionality, and project management behind application development. I am an advocate for students' well-rounded development in the studies of Science, Technology, Engineering, the Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM). a platform for students to develop personally and professionally through
engagement in student-run organizations and by utilizing Warrington’s

forward in my college career by providing mentors that challenge me to

Developing Cultural Intelligence Abroad, my Reflections on Dublin, Ireland.

What I did not expect, however, was a difference in way of life,
from the laissez-faire times stores opened and closed to the
ambiguity in how assignments were communicated and accomplished.

Throughout my past month exploring Ireland and adapting to their
work culture, I have gained the knowledge to recognize how different
groups try to understand each other, and how I can respond using
my cultural intelligence to break through the lens of a “single story”
and establish myself as a unique individual and valued coworker
in an interconnected world.
